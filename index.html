What is the Book of Mormon?

From the introduction of the Book of Mormon, "The Book of Mormon is a volume of holy scripture comparable to the Bible. It is a record of God's dealings with ancient inhabitants of the Americas and contains the fulness of the everlasting gospel." Members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints believe the Book of Mormon contains the teachings of Jesus Christ, and helps everyone who reads it to come closer to Him.

Scripture Reference: Introduction

Lehi's Vision in Jerusalem

Lehi received a revelation while in Jerusalem that the great city would be destroyed. Nobody believed him, and people tried to take his life because of how much they disagreed with him. Thankfully God protected him and his family. Lehi and His family left Jerusalem and fled to the wildnerness to avoid being destroyed.

Scripture Reference: 1 Nephi 1

Lehi and His Family's Journey In the Wilderness.

Lehi felt prompted to take his family into the wilderness to avoid destruction and to keep the promises of God alive. He brought the family of Ishmael with him to allow his family to continue.

Scripture Reference: 1 Nephi 2 - 1 Nephi 17

Crossing the Sea

Nephi was commanded to build a boat in order to cross the sea and make it to the promised land. Nephi and his family traveled across the sea. They faced trials along the way, but through their faith and trust in Jesus Christ they survived.

Scripture Reference: 1 Nephi 18

Splitting into Nephites and Lamanites

After the death of Lehi, Laman and Lemuel decided to split from Nephi and the other believers. They believed they were meant to lead Lehi's descendants since Laman was the firstborn.

Scripture Reference: 2 Nephi 5

Teachings of Christ

Different prophets (Lehi, Nephi, and Jacob) all profess their faith and knowledge of Jesus Christ. Lehi teaches his children (and grandchildren) the way to follow Christ, and delivers a powerful testimony of the Atonement. Both Nephi and Jacob teach their people about Christ and the consequences for not following Him.

Scripture Reference: 2 Nephi

King Benjamin's Sermon

King Benjamin is a righteous Nephi prophet who is teaching his people before his death. He teaches and instructs them in all things. He teaches them about Jesus Christ and His Atonement.

Scripture Reference: Mosiah 2-Mosiah 6

Abinadi/Alma

Abinadi is a Nephite prophet who is commanded to go and recover and convert an unrighteous king, his priests, and his people. Only one man listens, and his name is Alma. He eventually leads a revolution and helps to convert others to Christ and build up a church.

Scripture Reference: Mosiah 11-Alma 1

Alma/Amulek/Sons of Mosiah

The sons of King Mosiah and Alma try to lead others away from the church. After a revelation from an angel, Alma and the sons of Mosiah change and become powerful leaders in the church. They all lead missions to reclaim their lost brothers (both Nephites and Lamanites).

Scripture Reference: Mosiah 27-28; Alma 17-36

Samuel the Lamanite

He visits the Nephites while they're in a state of unbelief, and goes to reclaim them. He preaches about the impending coming of Christ, and teaches them what to look for and invites them to repent.

Scripture Reference: Helaman 13-16

Visit of Jesus Christ

Jesus Christ visits the ancient American continent to show them that He had fulfilled the Law of Moses, and that He was the Messiah. He taught them many things, which importantly revolved around His Atonement and his gospel.

Scripture Reference: 3 Nephi 9-28

Deterioration of the Nephite Nation

Less than 400 years after the coming of Christ, the once faithful Nephite nation fell into wickedness and sin which led to their destruction. They rejected and denied the visitation of Christ and His gospel.

Scripture Reference: 4 Nephi 1-Mormon 9; Moroni 9

Brother of Jared

Around the time of the Tower of Babel, a group of people found favor in the eyes of God and escaped to the ancient American Continent. They were guided by their leader, the Brother of Jared. Similar to the Nephite nation, they rejected the truth and saw the deterioration and destruction of their people.

Scripture Reference: Ether 1-3; 6

Moroni Sealing the Plates Away

After those 400 years, a prophet named Moroni saw he was one of the only followers of Christ left in the land. He saw that his time would soon come to an end, so he compiled all that he felt necessary and buried them for the future.

Scripture Reference: Moroni 10

Joseph Smith Finding the Plates

1400 years after Moroni buried the plates, a young farm boy was led to them after seeking the truth concerning God and salvation. He translated them through the gift and power of God into the Book of Mormon.

Scripture Reference: The Testimony of the Prophet Joseph Smith

My Testimony

The Book of Mormon means so much to me. The Book of Mormon has changed my life for the better. When I was younger, I remember reading through the Book of Mormon with my family. I didn’t take it seriously until I was a teenager in high school. I regret to say that I took it for granted for most of my life. I believed that the Church was true, Jesus was my Savior, and that Joseph Smith was a prophet. But a deeper knowledge and understanding of these truths didn’t come until later. I was blessed with some fantastic seminary teachers that bore the truthfulness of the Book of Mormon to me that helped to shape my testimony. I remember reading through the Book of Mormon for seminary, and that was the first time I had read through it. But on my mission was when I had a powerful experience reading it. My mission president gave us a 90 day Book of Mormon reading challenge so we would have read through the entirety of the Book of Mormon by the end of my first 2 transfers. I read through the Book of Mormon multiple times on my mission, and helped me to gain a great appreciation for my Savior. After learning Nepali and working with Nepali refugees, it helped me to understand and appreciate the simple truths found within those pages more and more. It helped me to understand how important it is to read it to gain a greater testimony of my Savior and His restored gospel.
